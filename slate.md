Got it. Quick note on the 42% stat before the rewrite: that figure is accurate but the framing your manager heard is off. It comes from a Delinea survey and refers to policyholders, not insurers - 42% of companies surveyed now have AI exclusions in their cyber policies. Here's the revised version:

---

**AI and Cyber Insurance: Key Developments**

**The Coverage Question**
- Generative AI lawsuits in the U.S. grew 978% from 2021 to 2025 [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91539281/corporate-insurers-are-starting-to-back-away-from-ai-risk) , pushing insurers to move fast on exclusions.
- Insurers are not broadly dropping cyber coverage. The carve-outs are happening in GL, D&O, and E&O lines. Berkshire Hathaway, Chubb, and Travelers won state regulator approval to remove AI-related damages from corporate policies. Berkley Insurance filed an absolute AI exclusion for D&O, E&O, and fiduciary lines covering "any actual or alleged use, deployment, or development of AI." [Adversa AI](https://adversa.ai/blog/ai-risk-management-insurance-what-the-new-exclusions-mean/)
- The stat circulating about "42% of insurers" is a misread. A Delinea survey found that 42% of respondents - meaning companies, not carriers - said their cyber policies now include exclusions tied to AI misuse and liability. [CSO Online](https://www.csoonline.com/article/4140230/ai-use-is-changing-how-much-companies-pay-for-cyber-insurance.html)
- Since January 1, 2026, Verisk/ISO endorsements have allowed carriers to remove generative AI exposure from commercial general liability policies, and at least six major carriers have filed their own AI exclusions with state regulators. [Substack](https://insuranceintel.substack.com/p/chubb-is-excluding-the-risk-its-own)

**Technical Incidents (Agentic AI, No Human Error)**
- Most cyber policies require a security breach to trigger coverage. An agentic AI loss - where an AI modifies records, deletes data, or executes transactions with no breach involved - falls outside that trigger entirely. [Healthsystemcio](https://healthsystemcio.com/2026/06/07/agentic-ai-insurance-gap/)
- Chubb has agreed to cover some AI-related incidents but specifically excludes events causing "widespread" simultaneous losses, citing systemic aggregated exposure as the concern. [The Rough Notes Company](https://roughnotes.com/ai-exclusions/)
- In 2026, some insurers introduced "AI Security Riders" requiring documented red-teaming and model-level risk assessments as prerequisites to coverage. [Compliancehub](https://compliancehub.wiki/cyber-insurance-ai-security-riders-2026-coverage-requirements/)

**Non-Technical Incidents (Hallucinations, Bad Outputs)**
- Real incidents are shaping policy: Air Canada was forced to honor a refund invented by its chatbot; Wolf River Electric sued Google after AI Overviews hallucinated non-existent legal troubles, causing a customer to cancel a contract. [Marketing AI Institute](https://www.marketingaiinstitute.com/blog/insurers-move-to-exclude-ai-risks)
- Many carriers are declining to write policies for AI-generated output claims because they cannot trace the reasoning path the AI took to reach a result. [CSO Online](https://www.csoonline.com/article/4159292/insurance-carriers-quietly-back-away-from-covering-ai-outputs.html)
- Absence of AI governance - no risk register, no model inventory - can now lead to coverage denials or inability to procure AI coverage at all. [Lexology](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=b76e0dba-d9a8-44f1-9f5d-6fbd0a22f6b6)

**AI on Defense = Lower Premiums**
- 86% of organizations report receiving premium discounts or credits for using AI-based security tools that strengthen their security posture. [CSO Online](https://www.csoonline.com/article/4140230/ai-use-is-changing-how-much-companies-pay-for-cyber-insurance.html)
- Companies implementing AI-powered threat detection alongside phishing-resistant MFA and EDR are seeing premium reductions of 20-50%. [EX NIHILO Magazine](https://exnihilomagazine.com/threat-detection-ai-cyber-insurance/)
- Insurers are scrutinizing how organizations implement and secure AI systems. Organizations rushing to adopt AI without proper controls face exclusions or claim denials - the same survey found AI weaknesses will likely be the next frontier in claims disputes. [Delinea](https://delinea.com/blog/cyber-insurance-trends-for-it-security-leaders-in-2026)
