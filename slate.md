Impact of AI on Cyber Insurance
•	Generative AI lawsuits in the U.S. grew 978% from 2021 to 2025, pushing insurers to move fast on exclusions. (FastCompany)
•	Insurers are not broadly dropping cyber coverage. The carve-outs are happening in GL, D&O, and E&O lines. Berkshire Hathaway, Chubb, and Travelers won state regulatory approval to remove AI-related damages from corporate policies. Berkley Insurance filed an absolute AI exclusion for D&O, E&O, and fiduciary lines covering "any actual or alleged use, deployment, or development of AI." (Adversa AI)
•	Insurers are scrutinizing how organizations implement and secure AI systems. Organizations rushing to adopt AI without proper controls face exclusions or claim denials - the same survey found AI weaknesses will likely be the next frontier in claims disputes.
•	A Delinea survey found that 42% of respondents (meaning companies, not carriers) said their cyber policies now include exclusions tied to AI misuse and liability. (CSO Online)
Technical Incidents (Agentic AI, No/Negligible Human Error)
o	Most cyber policies require a security breach to trigger coverage. An agentic AI loss where an AI modifies records, deletes data, or executes transactions with no breach involved - falls outside that trigger entirely. (Health System CIO)
o	Chubb has agreed to cover some AI-related incidents but specifically excludes events causing "widespread" simultaneous losses, citing systemic aggregated exposure as the concern. (RoughNotes)
o	In 2026, some insurers introduced "AI Security Riders" requiring documented red-teaming and model-level risk assessments as prerequisites to coverage. (Compliance Hub)
Non-Technical Incidents (Hallucinations, Bad Outputs)
o	Real incidents are shaping policy: Air Canada was forced to honour a refund invented by its chatbot; Wolf River Electric sued Google after AI Overviews hallucinated non-existent legal troubles, causing a customer to cancel a contract. (Marketing AI Institute)
o	Many carriers are declining to write policies for AI-generated output claims because they cannot trace the reasoning path the AI took to reach a result.
o	Absence of AI governance (no risk register, no model inventory) can now lead to coverage denials or inability to procure AI coverage at all. (Lexology)

•	86% of organizations report receiving premium discounts or credits for using AI-based security tools that strengthen their security posture.
•	Companies implementing AI-powered threat detection alongside phishing-resistant MFA and EDR are seeing premium reductions of 20-50%. (EX Nihilo)
